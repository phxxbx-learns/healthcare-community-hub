📝 Formal Letters in Healthcare

Formal letters are essential communication tools in hospitals and healthcare institutions. They convey official requests, referrals, endorsements, and responses with proper authority and professionalism.

📨
Referral Letter
Refer patients to specialists or facilities
▼

A referral letter formally transfers a patient's care to another healthcare provider or facility. It must include patient details, diagnosis, reason for referral, and relevant medical history.

  • Use official hospital letterhead
  • Include patient's full name, age, and diagnosis
  • State the purpose of referral clearly
  • Include attending physician's signature
📩
Request Letter
Request supplies, equipment, or services
▼

Request letters are used to formally ask for resources, services, schedules, or assistance from another department or external entity.

  • Be specific about what is being requested
  • Justify the reason for the request
  • Indicate urgency if applicable
  • Provide contact information for follow-up
📜
Endorsement Letter
Endorse staff, services, or procedures
▼

Endorsement letters officially support a person, policy, product, or procedure. They are signed by an authority figure and carry official weight.

  • State clearly what or who is being endorsed
  • Provide supporting reasons and qualifications
  • Use authoritative, professional language
  • Include signatory's position and credentials
💬
Patient Concern Response
Address complaints and patient feedback
▼

This type of letter responds to formal complaints or concerns raised by patients or their families, maintaining empathy while upholding institutional standards.

  • Acknowledge the concern respectfully
  • Avoid defensive or dismissive language
  • State actions taken to address the issue
  • Express commitment to quality care

💡 Quick Writing Tips

  • Always use official letterhead with complete institutional information
  • Keep language formal, concise, and free of medical jargon when writing to non-clinicians
  • Double-check spelling of patient names, diagnoses, and medication names
  • Include your full name, credentials, and designation in the signature block

✅ Common Mistakes to Avoid

✅ Best Practices
  • Use complete, professional salutations (Dear Dr. Santos)
  • State your purpose in the first paragraph
  • Maintain a courteous and respectful tone throughout
  • Include all necessary contact details for follow-up
  • Sign with full name, credentials, and designation
  • Proofread for spelling, grammar, and factual accuracy
❌ Common Errors
  • Using casual greetings like "Hi" or "Hey" in formal letters
  • Leaving out the date or recipient's address
  • Using vague language that obscures the request
  • Writing in ALL CAPS for emphasis — use bold instead
  • Forgetting to attach referenced documents
  • Using personal email accounts for official correspondence

📋 Hospital Memoranda

Memoranda (memos) are the backbone of internal hospital communication. They are used for staff notices, policy updates, schedule reminders, and official advisories within the organization.

🏥 BICOL MEDICAL CENTER
Nursing Service Division · Internal Communication
MEMORANDUM
TO:All Nursing Staff, Ward 3 & 4
FROM:Maria B. Reyes, RN, MAN — Chief Nurse
DATE:May 5, 2025
SUBJECT:MANDATORY STAFF MEETING — Hand Hygiene Compliance Audit

This is to inform all nursing personnel in Wards 3 and 4 that a mandatory staff meeting will be held on May 10, 2025, at 2:00 PM in the Nursing Conference Room, 2nd Floor.

The agenda includes: (1) review of the recent hand hygiene compliance audit results, (2) identification of areas for improvement, and (3) refresher on standard infection prevention protocols per DOH guidelines.

Attendance is strictly required. Staff on duty shall coordinate with the ward supervisor for relief coverage. Please bring your nursing notes and last month's incident reports.

Non-attendance without valid excuse will be noted in the personnel file.

 
MARIA B. REYES, RN, MAN
Chief Nurse, Bicol Medical Center

Key Elements of a Hospital Memo

📌 TO: Recipient(s) clearly identified
📌 FROM: Sender's name and position
📌 DATE: Date of issuance
📌 SUBJECT: Concise, all-caps topic
📌 BODY: Clear, organized paragraphs
📌 SIGNATURE: With name and designation
📌 DISTRIBUTION: Cc list if applicable

📧 Professional Emails in Healthcare

Professional emails are the fastest form of official written communication in modern healthcare settings. Proper email etiquette protects patient confidentiality and maintains institutional credibility.

New Message — Official Healthcare Email
FROM: nursing@bicolmedicalcenter.ph
TO: ward4supervisor@bicolmedicalcenter.ph
SUBJECT: Shift Endorsement — Night Duty to Morning Duty, May 15, 2025
CC: chiefrn@bicolmedicalcenter.ph

Good morning, Ma'am/Sir,

I am submitting the endorsement report for the night shift (10:00 PM – 6:00 AM) in Ward 4 for your reference.

Total patients: 18 (3 new admissions, 2 discharges)

Critical patients: (1) Patient in Bed 4-B with worsening dyspnea — O2 therapy at 4L/min ongoing, attending physician notified. IV line in Bed 4-F replaced at 3:00 AM. All medications administered as ordered.

Please ensure follow-up on pending blood results for Beds 4-A and 4-C.

Respectfully,
RN Ana Mariz Villanueva
Ward 4 Night Duty Nurse

CONFIDENTIALITY NOTICE: This email may contain privileged and confidential patient information intended solely for the named recipient(s).

📖 Email Etiquette Rules

📌
Clear Subject Lines
Always state the topic, ward, and date. Example: "Shift Endorsement – Ward 4 – May 15, 2025"
🤝
Professional Greetings
Use "Good morning/afternoon" or "Dear Dr./Ma'am/Sir" — never "Hey" or "Yo"
🔒
Confidentiality Reminder
Include a confidentiality notice at the bottom for emails containing patient information
✅
Professional Closing
End with "Respectfully," "Sincerely," or "Best regards," followed by full name and designation
📎
Attachments
Mention all attachments in the body. Use descriptive filenames like "MedRec_JuanDelaCruz_05152025.pdf"
⚡
Reply Promptly
Healthcare emails require timely responses. Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours even if full reply takes longer

Best Practices

💡 Writing Tips for Healthcare Professionals

Apply these essential writing principles to every formal communication you produce in a healthcare setting.

✂️
Be Concise
Say exactly what needs to be said — no more. Avoid verbose phrases like "in order to" (use "to") or "at this point in time" (use "now").
🎩
Maintain Professionalism
Use formal language at all times. Avoid casual speech, jokes, sarcasm, and emotional language in official communications.
🚫
Avoid Slang & Jargon
Write for clarity. Avoid medical shorthand, abbreviations (unless standard), and slang that may confuse or offend recipients.
🔒
Observe Confidentiality
Never include sensitive patient information in unsecured emails. Follow RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act) and your institution's privacy protocols.
📐
Use Proper Formatting
Consistent font, proper margins, correct salutations, and a structured layout show professionalism and respect for the reader.
🔍
Proofread Always
Read your document at least twice before sending. Check spelling, grammar, accuracy of facts (names, dates, drug dosages), and formatting.
🎯
State Purpose Early
The first paragraph must clearly state why you are writing. Readers in healthcare settings are busy — get to the point immediately.
📅
Include All Key Details
Always include dates, times, names (spelled correctly), reference numbers, and contact information so the reader can act without guessing.
🤲
Use Respectful Language
Acknowledge the recipient's authority and expertise. Phrases like "We respectfully request" and "Kindly advise" show courtesy and cooperation.
